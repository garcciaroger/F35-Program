Stealth Technology Briefing

F-35 low observable technology overview

The Science of Invisibility

The F-35's stealth capabilities derive from advanced design features and materials that significantly reduce radar, infrared, acoustic, and visual detection signatures. Unlike conventional aircraft, the F-35 was designed with stealth as a core requirement from its inception.

With a radar cross-section (RCS) comparable to a metal golf ball, the F-35 can penetrate sophisticated enemy air defense systems undetected where legacy fighters cannot survive.

Internal Weapons Bay

Conceals weapons internally to eliminate radar-reflective external stores, maintaining stealth during combat missions.

Edge Alignment

Surface edges are precisely aligned at similar angles to redirect radar energy away from receivers.

Advanced Materials

Fiber mat skin and radar-absorbing materials (RAM) absorb and diffuse radar energy rather than reflecting it.

IR Signature Reduction

Advanced cooling systems and specialized coatings reduce the aircraft's infrared signature to counter thermal detection.

ADVANCED AVIONICS & SENSOR FUSION

Revolutionary integrated sensor package providing unmatched situational awareness

Complete Battlespace Awareness

The F-35's avionics suite represents a revolution in fighter aircraft capability, integrating advanced sensors, communications, and processing power to deliver unprecedented situational awareness.

Unlike traditional fighters that manage sensors independently, the F-35's core processor fuses data from all onboard and off-board sensors into a single, integrated picture of the battlespace, reducing pilot workload and increasing decision speed.

THE EDGE
"The F-35 doesn't just have sensors - it has an integrated sensor package that works as a single system, giving pilots information superiority unlike any aircraft before it."

Core Sensor Systems

AN/APG-81 AESA Radar

Advanced electronically scanned array radar with enhanced range, resolution, and jamming resistance, providing air-to-air and air-to-ground capabilities simultaneously.

Distributed Aperture System

Six infrared cameras providing 360° spherical coverage for missile warning, aircraft detection, and night vision, allowing pilots to "see through" the aircraft structure.

Electro-Optical Targeting

Integrated forward-looking infrared (FLIR) and infrared search and track (IRST) capability with air-to-air and air-to-ground targeting, laser designation, and range finding.

Gen III Helmet Display

Advanced helmet-mounted display projecting critical flight data and sensor imagery directly onto the pilot's visor, enabling weapons targeting by simply looking at objectives.

Advanced Display Technology

GEN III HELMET DISPLAY SYSTEM

Revolutionizing pilot interface with unprecedented situational awareness and targeting capabilities

$400,000 Per Unit

Each helmet is custom-fitted to individual pilots using precision laser scanning

Beyond Human Vision

The F-35's Gen III Helmet Mounted Display System (HMDS) replaces traditional head-up displays, projecting all essential flight data directly onto the pilot's visor. This revolutionary interface extends human capability by providing 360-degree situational awareness day or night.

With integrated night vision and the ability to "see through" the aircraft via the Distributed Aperture System, pilots can look down at the floor and see the ground beneath them as if the aircraft were transparent.

360° Vision

Integrates with DAS to provide spherical visibility around the aircraft, allowing pilots to detect threats in any direction.

Look & Shoot Targeting

Enables weapon aiming through head movement—pilots can lock onto targets simply by looking at them.

Integrated Night Vision

Built-in night vision eliminates the need for separate goggles, reducing weight and providing superior low-light capability.

Custom-Fitted Design

Precisely tailored to each pilot's head dimensions using laser scanning technology for perfect fit and display alignment.
