F-35A CTOL

CONVENTIONAL TAKEOFF AND LANDING

The F-35A is the conventional takeoff and landing (CTOL) variant designed for the U.S. Air Force and other air forces. It represents the most balanced 5th-generation fighter in the world.

This variant combines advanced stealth with supersonic speed, agility, and a comprehensive integrated sensor package.

STOVL VARIANT

F-35B STOVL

SHORT TAKEOFF/VERTICAL LANDING

The F-35B is the short takeoff/vertical landing (STOVL) variant designed for the U.S. Marine Corps and international partners requiring expeditionary capabilities.

This revolutionary variant combines stealth technology with the ability to operate from austere bases and a range of ships.

CARRIER VARIANT

F-35C CV

CARRIER VARIANT

The F-35C is the U.S. Navy's first stealth fighter and the world's only 5th Generation, long-range stealth strike fighter designed specifically for aircraft carrier operations.

With its larger wings and robust landing gear, the F-35C offers exceptional slow-speed approach handling for carrier landings.

FIRST COMBAT MISSION

The F-35 saw its first combat use with the Israeli Air Force, making the "Adir" the first F-35 worldwide to participate in operational strike missions. Shortly after, USMC F-35Bs conducted strikes in Afghanistan.

MISSION DETAILS

In May 2018, Israeli Air Force Major General Amikam Norkin announced that Israel had used its F-35I "Adir" fighters to conduct airstrikes on at least two occasions, making Israel the first country to use the F-35 in combat operations.

Following Israel's use, the United States Marine Corps F-35Bs deployed with the 13th Marine Expeditionary Unit conducted their first combat strikes against Taliban targets in Afghanistan on September 27, 2018.
